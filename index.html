Aikido

Eskilstuna Ki-Aikido

A Japanese martial art performed by flowing with the motion of the attacker rather than opposing it straight-on. This requires much less physical strength, as the practitioner directs the attacker's momentum with entering and turning motions, followed by various throws or joint locks.

A relatively modern Japanese martial art. The goal of judo is to either throw or takedown one's opponent to the ground and immobilize or subdue them with a grappling maneuver, joint lock, strangle hold, or choke.

Taekwondo

Eskilstuna Taekwondo

The Korean art of self-defense. Training involves learning a system of blocks, kicks, punches, and open-handed strikes, as well as varying forms of take-downs, throws, and joint locks, all of which develop strength, speed, balance, flexibility, and stamina.
